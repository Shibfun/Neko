NekoToken Whitepaper - Shibarium Edition

1. Executive Summary

NekoToken (NEKO) is an ERC-20 token deployed on Shibarium, a layer-2 blockchain with a 5-second block time, launched on March 22, 2025. It introduces a dual-staking ecosystem where users can stake the native NEKO token and the external FEED token (0xe9Cb2D7ADC24Fc59FE00D6C0A0669BDF16805Fe0) to earn NEKO rewards. With a total supply cap of 1 quadrillion tokens, NekoToken employs a detailed reward reduction mechanism to ensure sustainability over time.

This whitepaper provides an in-depth look at the tokenomics, staking rules, and the reward reduction process tailored to Shibarium’s fast block production.

3. Staking Mechanics

3.1 FEED Staking

  • Stake Amount: Fixed at 10 billion FEED (10,000,000,000 * 10^18).
  • Process: Call stakeFeed(). One stake per address.
  • Unstaking: Call unstakeFeed() to retrieve FEED and claim NEKO rewards.
  • Reward Claim: claimFeedRewards() for NEKO without unstaking.

3.2 NEKO Staking

  • Stake Amount: Multiples of 10,000 NEKO (10,000 * 10^18).
  • Process: Call stakeNeko(amount). Additional staking allowed.
  • Unstaking: Call unstakeNeko() to withdraw all NEKO and claim rewards.
  • Reward Claim: claimNekoRewards() for NEKO without unstaking.

4.2 Detailed Reward Reduction Mechanism

NekoToken employs a linear reward reduction mechanism to gradually decrease rewards over time, ensuring a balanced emission rate. The reduction is tied to Shibarium’s block production (5 seconds/block) and governed by the following constants:

  • Decay Period: 600,000 blocks (~34.7 days).
  • Maximum Decay Point: 29,400,000 blocks (~4.66 years).
  • Base Formula: Base = 50 - (Elapsed Blocks / 600,000).
  • Reward Formula: Current Reward = (Initial Reward * Base) / 50.
  • Minimum Threshold: When Base ≤ 1 (after 29,400,000 blocks), rewards stabilize at the minimum rate.

4.3 Step-by-Step Reduction Process

The reward reduction occurs in discrete steps every 600,000 blocks. Here’s how it works:

  1. Initial Phase (0 blocks): Base = 50, FEED reward = 500 NEKO/block, NEKO reward = 0.025 NEKO/block per 10,000 NEKO.
  2. After 600,000 blocks (~34.7 days): Base = 49, FEED reward = 490 NEKO/block, NEKO reward = 0.0245 NEKO/block per 10,000 NEKO.
  3. After 6,000,000 blocks (~347 days): Base = 40, FEED reward = 400 NEKO/block, NEKO reward = 0.02 NEKO/block per 10,000 NEKO.
  4. After 29,400,000 blocks (~4.66 years): Base = 1, FEED reward = 10 NEKO/block, NEKO reward = 0.00005 NEKO/block per 10,000 NEKO.
  5. Beyond 30,000,000 blocks (~4.75 years): Base ≤ 1, rewards remain at minimum rates until the 1 quadrillion cap is reached.

4.4 Time Calculations on Shibarium

With Shibarium’s 5-second block time:

  • 600,000 blocks: 600,000 * 5 = 3,000,000 seconds ≈ 34.7 days.
  • 29,400,000 blocks: 29,400,000 * 5 = 147,000,000 seconds ≈ 4.66 years (1,702 days).
  • 20,000 blocks (Max Accumulation): 20,000 * 5 = 100,000 seconds ≈ 27.8 hours.

Example: A user staking 10 billion FEED earns 500 NEKO/block initially. After ~347 days (6M blocks), this drops to 400 NEKO/block. By ~4.66 years (29.4M blocks), it stabilizes at 10 NEKO/block.
