By combining staking opportunities with the external FEED token (0xe9Cb2D7ADC24Fc59FE00D6C0A0669BDF16805Fe0) and the native NEKO token, NekoToken offers a unique dual-staking model designed to reward long-term participation and network stability.

Deployed on March 22, 2025, NekoToken leverages Ethereum’s robust infrastructure to provide a transparent, secure, and sustainable staking platform. This whitepaper details the tokenomics, staking rules, reward mechanics, and vision for the NekoToken ecosystem.

3. Staking Ecosystem

3.1 FEED Token Staking

FEED staking allows holders of the FEED token to participate in the NekoToken ecosystem by locking a fixed amount of FEED to earn NEKO rewards.

  • Required Amount: 10 billion FEED (10,000,000,000 * 10^18).
  • Staking Process: Call stakeFeed() to transfer 10 billion FEED to the contract. Only one stake per address is permitted.
  • Unstaking: Call unstakeFeed() to retrieve FEED and claim NEKO rewards.
  • Reward Claiming: Use claimFeedRewards() to claim NEKO without unstaking.
Example: Alice stakes 10 billion FEED on day 1. After 1,000 blocks (~3.3 hours at 12s/block), she earns 500,000 NEKO (500 NEKO/block initially). She can claim this anytime or unstake to retrieve her FEED.

3.2 NEKO Token Staking

NEKO staking encourages holders to lock their tokens, enhancing network longevity and rewarding commitment.

  • Minimum Unit: 10,000 NEKO (10,000 * 10^18), with staking amounts in multiples of this unit.
  • Staking Process: Call stakeNeko(amount) to lock NEKO. Users can add to their stake over time.
  • Unstaking: Call unstakeNeko() to withdraw all staked NEKO and claim rewards.
  • Reward Claiming: Use claimNekoRewards() to claim NEKO without unstaking.
Example: Bob stakes 50,000 NEKO (5 units). After 1,000 blocks, he earns 125 NEKO (0.025 NEKO/block * 5 units * 1,000 blocks). He can later stake more NEKO to increase his rewards.

4.3 Reward Decay

Rewards decay over time to ensure long-term sustainability:

  • Decay Period: 600,000 blocks (~2.3 months).
  • Formula: Reward = (Initial Rate * Base) / 50, where Base = 50 - (Blocks / 600,000).
  • End Point: After 29,400,000 blocks (~11 years), rewards stabilize at the minimum rate.
Example: After 6,000,000 blocks (~2.3 years), FEED rewards drop to ~400 NEKO/block (Base = 40). After 29,400,000 blocks (~11 years), it’s fixed at 10 NEKO/block.

5. Tokenomics & Mining

NEKO tokens are minted through staking rewards with a clear cap:

  • Total Supply Cap: 1 quadrillion NEKO.
  • Mining Duration: Continues until the cap is reached or indefinitely at minimum rates post-29,400,000 blocks.
  • End Trigger: Mining halts when total supply hits 1 quadrillion, signaled by the MiningEnded event.
